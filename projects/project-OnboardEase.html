OnboardEase

  • WebApp
  • InteractionDesign
  • Co-Designing

OnboardEase is a platform for organizations with dynamic workforces to onboard employees.

It systematically presents organization-based and role-specific knowledge while offering a simulated workspace for new employees to practice and learn their work.

Team
Christy Fang, Chun Hin Tsang, Sylvia Zhang (myself), Vito Fan
My Role
UX Researcher, UI/UX Designer
Tools
Figma, Miro
Duration
3 Month

Context

MeaningfulWork and its operational challenges

MeaningfulWork, a Vancouver-based startup, offers a platform connecting volunteers with volunteering opportunities. During our introductory meeting with the CEO, we discovered that the company's frequent employee changes necessitated efficient onboarding. However, the complexity of operational tasks made the onboarding process time-consuming.

How might we streamline the onboarding process to accommodate frequent employee changes and help employees effectively learn new tasks?

Discover

Analyzing onboarding process

To identify pain points in the onboarding process and opportunities to improve the interaction experience, my team created journey maps of the new employee and the manager responsible for training. I mapped out the emotion and thoughts of the new employee by asking my teammate to act as an intern and think out loud.

User journey maps revealed that new employees found onboarding overwhelming due to a lack of a systematic view of their responsibilities. Additionally, they faced difficulties concentrating on learning materials while navigating multiple platforms to complete their tasks.

Co-Designing with managers and employees

To harnesses diverse perspectives, promote innovation, and ensure intended users' needs and preferences are met, my team hosted a co-design workshop with managers and interns from MeaningfulWork's team. After defining concrete goals for the workshop, I planned the participatory session with my team. to choose and arrange activities for. Activities were chosen and adapted based on our goals, ranging from role-playing activities, analytical activities and creative activities. I structured the session in a way that the previous activity will prepare the participants for the next activity.

Besides leading one of the participatory activities during the workshop, I also summarized insights from the workshop:

  1. New employees seek instant access to reliable training resources, while managers also strive to offer easily digestible learning materials that provide access to expertise for everyone.
  2. Dense information coupled with non-linear processes poses challenges for employees in retaining information during onboarding.
  3. Managers hope to foster an open environment where new employees can provide feedback and communicate their needs.

Define

Narrowing down the focus

While we gained multiple insights from interviews and workshops, we could not manage to address all the issues within the scope of our project. We chose to put the need for an open environment for communication aside since that can be achieved by adapting existing communication platforms. Therefore, the design problem was reframed to:

How might we design an onboarding solution that provides a systematic view of knowledge and streamlines navigation for new employees to overcome challenges of overwhelming information and non-linear processes?

Develop

Initial Concepts

Inspired by the pre-existing UX design of digital products to onboard users, I proposed a concept that creates a simulated workspace for employees to step through their learning tasks with visual guidance.

Another concept of helping employees to understand the team's operational structure was developed by my teammates.

Feedbacks

We presented our concepts to our instructor and CEO of MeaningfulWork and received two major feedbacks. The first one is the two concepts can be combined into one. The second feedback is that the first concept can include interfaces to create and update learning guides.

The second feedback was very valuable because it looked at the experience from the managers' point of view and address the need for frequent workflow updates within MeaningfulWork's team. Thus I took the responsibility to build an interface for updating the onboard guide.

Deliver

Final solution

After incorporating feedback, the final product, OnboardEase, is an aggregated learning guide systematically presenting technical knowledge. With a simulated workspace, it enables new employees to familiarize themselves with tools and processes without being anxious or confused. It also enables easy editing of onboarding materials.

New user journey

Introduction to OnboardEase

New employees receive a link to the learning guide during the onboarding meeting with the manager.

Upon logging into OnboardEase, they are greeted and directed to learning materials.

Onboarding Overview

New employees enter learning modules for their roles and gain an overview of their responsibilities.

Employees can learn at their own pace and review when necessary.

Learning New Tasks

With the hands-on task simulation on OnboardEase, the new employee can learn and practice their tasks in one place.

This reduces their anxiety about making mistakes and saves the hassle of shifting between video tutorials and operation platforms.

Adding New Tutorial

OnboardEase allows easy documentation of training materials.

This saves managers to repeat the same procedure every time a new employee joins the team or a horizontal movement takes place and keeps training materials up to date.

Reflection

Co-designing as a way to research and innovate

From this project, I not only learned how to plan and run a participatory workshop but also had a taste of co-design methods. Unlike traditional research methods, such as interviews and surveys, the workshop encouraged active stakeholder participation, leading to user-centred and contextually relevant solutions and fostering a sense of ownership and commitment.

Consideration of feasibility of design solution

While MeaningfulWork's team expressed their appreciation for our final solution in the exit meeting, my team missed the opportunity to help MeaningfulWork easily adapt our solution. Since what we presented was a mockup and MeaningfulWork did not have the budget to develop such a platform, a substitute approach was needed for them to achieve the same interaction flow. We could have researched existing platforms and designed a minimum viable product that is ready to be used by MeaningfulWork.

In my future practice, I would like to consider ways to actively engage my target users and provides opportunities for them to dive into the design problem and create the solution. I will also take my client and target users' capabilities into account when delivering the final solution.
